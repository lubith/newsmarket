Understanding Deepfake Technology

Deepfake technology relies on machine learning algorithms to manipulate video and audio content. By using a technique called generative adversarial networks (GANs), creators can produce realistic-looking videos that can depict individuals saying or doing things they never actually did. This can lead to various applications, from harmless entertainment to malicious uses.

How Deepfakes are Created

  • Data Collection: A large dataset of images and videos of the target individual is required.
  • Training the Model: The AI model is trained using the collected data to learn the target's facial expressions, voice, and mannerisms.
  • Video Generation: The trained model can then produce new videos by superimposing the target's likeness onto existing footage.
